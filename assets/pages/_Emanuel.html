Google's Page Rank Algorithm



Google co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin unveiled an algorithm that would completely change how people could retrieve information from the internet in the early 2000s. PageRank evaluated the authority and relevancy of online pages by examining their inbound links, using the link analysis idea as its foundation. This invention revolutionized web navigation by giving users more precise and pertinent search results. Google's search engine has evolved into a necessary tool in today's world, influencing the way we regularly access and use information.


DeepBlue vs Kasparov:



The current world chess champion, Garry Kasparov, faced up against IBM's Deep Blue, a specially designed supercomputer, in a historic encounter in 1997. The notable battle was won by Deep Blue, which amply demonstrated the remarkable powers of artificial intelligence. This was an important moment in the development of artificial intelligence, which proved that machines could do more complex cognitive tasks than humans (even though the best chess players nowadays still manage to win sometimes). This accomplishment then sparked advancements in deep and machine learning, which accelerated the spread of AI applications in a variety of industries, including healthcare and finance.


Blockchain Technology



The decentralised and immutable ledger system known as blockchain technology was first made public in 2009 with the introduction of Bitcoin by Satoshi Nakamoto. Blockchain has the ability to completely transform sectors like finance (although current controversies partially suggest otherwise), supply chain management, and healthcare that depend on trust and transparency. As such, its influence goes far beyond cryptocurrencies. The fundamental cryptographic ideas of blockchain offer a fresh approach to the long-standing issue of safe, substantiable digital transactions.


CRISPR-Cas9



Although it does not completely fall under the purview of computer science, computational ability is closely related to the development of CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing technology. Thanks to this ground-breaking instrument created by Emmanuelle Charpentier and Jennifer Doudna, scientists are now able to edit genetic material with previously unthinkable precision. Computational algorithms play a main role in the design and analysis of CRISPR, showcasing the mutually beneficial relationship between computer science and the biological sciences. This discovery has enormous promise for improving our knowledge of genetics and treating genetic illnesses, especially as development continues in the future.
